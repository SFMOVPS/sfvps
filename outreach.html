Our office, the Department of Children, Youth, and their Families, and the Department of Public Health run the the Street Violence Intervention Program (SVIP). The Street Violence Intervention Program aims to reduce street violence and homicides through street-level prevention and intervention. The SVIP focuses especially on young people involved in street violence.

Street Outreach

SVIP works in neighborhoods including the Tenderloin, Mission/Excelsior, and Visitacion Valley. SVIP workers develop strong relationships within their assigned communities, particularly with young people and their families. Through regular canvassing, workers defuse potentially violent situations in schools and neighborhood "hot zones". Workers also visit detention centers such as the Youth Guidance Center and the Hall of Justice. SVIP workers use their relationships and influence with youth to prevent and intervene in incidents that could lead to serious street violence.

Crisis Response

When a homicide or shooting occurs, SVIP workers, together with the police and the Department of Public Health's Crisis Response team, respond to the crisis both at the crime scene and at San Francisco General Hospital. The team uses their expertise to de-escalate the situation and prevent retaliation.

Community Mobilization

SVIP workers organize events to bring people from their neighborhood together. Events include workshops, recreation activities, and rallies. These events provide an opportunity for community members to discuss and plan how to change their community for the better. The events can also reduce tensions within the neighborhood.

Through street outreach, crisis response, and community mobilization activities the SVIP makes San Francisco safer and healthier for all its residents.
