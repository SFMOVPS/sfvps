What We Do

Many partners within city government have a role in preventing, responding to, and reducing street and family violence. The Mayor's Office of Violence Prevention Services coordinates between city departments, community, and faith-based organizations.

Through the vision and leadership of Mayor Edwin M. Lee, our office develops short and long term goals that promote public safety. Through our coordination of diverse city and community-based public safety partners, our office works to ensure that:

    • Collaborate with city and local organizations to provide immediate comprehensive and well-coordinated services and support to individuals impacted by street violence
    • Develop long-term strategies promoting public safety through early intervention, education, workforce, behavioral health, diversion, reentry services, street outreach and intervention, and crisis response services

Ensuring Support for Victims

Under the leadership of Mayor Edwin Lee, San Francisco experienced a reduction in street violence with a 36% in homicides; however, our work is not complete until we ensure the wellbeing of communities, families, and children across San Francisco.

The focus of our work emphasizes the importance of city and county coordination of city agencies to direct immediate access of services for individuals impacted by street violence during the moment of crisis. The Citywide Crisis Response system is a collaborative partnership with San Francisco Police Department and Department of Public Healthâ€™s Comprehensive Crisis Response Services (CCS) and San Francisco Street Violence Intervention Program (SVIP) who collectively support individuals in the event of a crisis. Collaborative partners also include the San Francisco General Hospital Trauma Center, Emergency Department Social Workers, The Wraparound Project, and the Victim Services Unit with the Office of the District Attorney.

As an extension of the Crisis Response Network, the Street Violence Response Team activates after a shooting, critical assault, and or homicide and begins immediately identifying intervention and prevention strategies. The team comprised of representatives from city, community, and faith-based organizations coordinates comprehensive enforcement, prevention and intervention strategies at the enforcement, individual, family, and community level.

Through a holistic trauma-informed approach the team identifies the immediate needs for the individual impacted by violence, the perpetrators of violence, the family and the community. The team makes sure that the victims of these incidents receive necessary support services.
