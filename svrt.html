Street Violence Response Team

The Street Violence Response Team is a collaboration between city departments, community, and faith-based organizations. The team identifies immediate, individualized services for individuals impacted by street and family violence.

Coordination

  • Street Violence Response Team

Purpose

The Street Violence Response Team directs immediate services and support to direct and indirect victims of crime. Direct victims are individuals who are harmed or injured as a result of a crime. Indirect victims include families, friends, and witnesses to crime. The team connects victims to services such as legal, financial, relocation, and mental health support services.

The Numbers

The Street Violence Response Team operates to plan an immediate violence intervention response for any neighborhood impacted by a critical street violence incident and/or homicide(s). In 2014, the team developed 178 response plans delivering a diverse array of community-based services to 240 direct victims and an additional 265 victims impacted by street and family violence.

Who We Serve

  • 80% of street and family violence victims are African American and Latino
  • 45% of victims are young, age 18–25

Team Members

Members of the Street Violence Response Team include representatives from the Mayor’s Office, Board of Supervisors, San Francisco Police Department, Adult Probation Department, Juvenile Probation Department, Department of Public Health-CRT, Housing Authority, District Attorney’s Office, San Francisco Unified School District, Urban Strategies, Street Violence Intervention Program, and faith-based leaders.
